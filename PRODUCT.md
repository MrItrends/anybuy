# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Young working-class Nigerians (18-35) in Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt. Buyers want fast, safe secondhand deals without getting scammed. Sellers want a trusted place to move items quickly. Riders want flexible income using their motorcycle or bicycle. All users are mobile-first, price-conscious, and skeptical of digital platforms until trust is earned.

## Product Purpose

AnyBuy is Nigeria's trusted secondhand marketplace with live selling, escrow protection, and local delivery. It exists to make resale safe, social, and instant. Success means a buyer in Lagos can watch a seller drop a product live, bid on it, and have it at their door the same day via an AnyBuy rider.

## Brand Personality

Bold, warm, street-smart. "Hustle with trust."

## Anti-references

- Jumia / Konga: corporate, cold, cluttered marketplace feel
- Generic fintech dark-mode with neon green accents
- Editorial-typographic (display serif + italic drop caps + broadsheet grid) — wrong lane for a consumer marketplace
- Western gig-economy rider pages (Deliveroo green, DoorDash red) — too polished and cold, wrong cultural register

## Design Principles

1. **Trust first** — Every surface should reduce hesitation. Escrow, verification badges, and transparency are features, not footnotes.
2. **Energy without noise** — The brand is high-energy but never chaotic. Orange carries the heat; dark grounds it.
3. **Local by default** — Nigerian faces, Nigerian cities, Nigerian context. No stock imagery that could be anywhere.
4. **Mobile is the primary canvas** — Design for 375px first. Large-screen layouts are enhancements.
5. **Show the people** — AnyBuy is a people business. Real humans (buyers, sellers, riders) make it credible.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Strong color contrast ratios on orange backgrounds. Support for low-bandwidth connections (optimized images, no video autoplay). Right-to-left not required.
